Nintendo President Shuntaro Furukawa recently addressed concerns regarding the rising costs of memory components and their potential impact on the pricing of the upcoming Switch 2. Despite the ongoing surge in RAM priceslargely driven by demand from AI data centersFurukawa reassured shareholders that there are currently no plans to increase the price of the Switch 2. He emphasized that Nintendo is actively engaging with suppliers to secure a stable supply of components in the long term.

Furukawa acknowledged that while the recent memory price hikes have not significantly affected hardware profitability in the short term, the company remains vigilant. Should these costs continue to rise into the next financial year, Nintendo will reassess its pricing strategy. He noted that any decision regarding price adjustments would consider various factors, including market conditions, sales trends, and overall platform adoption.

As the gaming landscape evolves, it will be interesting to see how Nintendo navigates these challenges while maintaining its competitive edge. Will they manage to keep the Switch 2 affordable for gamers, or will rising costs force a price increase in the future?
